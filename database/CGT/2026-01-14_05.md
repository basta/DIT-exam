# Question
(5 pts) Find a symmetric equilibrium in a first-price sealed-bid auction with 5 bidders. Private values $v$ are drawn independently from $F(v) = v^3$ on $[0, 1]$. [cite: 319, 320, 321]

---

# Solution
The optimal bidding strategy is $b(v) = E[Y | Y < v] = v - \int_{0}^{v} \frac{F_Y(x)}{F_Y(v)} dx$. [cite: 327]
1. **Opponent's Max CDF:** The highest value among the 4 opponents is $F_Y(x) = F(x)^4 = (x^3)^4 = x^{12}$. [cite: 327]
2. **Calculation:**
   $$b(v) = v - \frac{1}{v^{12}} \int_{0}^{v} x^{12} dx$$ [cite: 329]
   $$b(v) = v - \frac{1}{v^{12}} \left[ \frac{x^{13}}{13} \right]_0^v = v - \frac{v^{13}}{13v^{12}}$$ [cite: 329]
   $$b(v) = v - \frac{v}{13} = \frac{12}{13}v$$ [cite: 329]

**Final Result:** The symmetric equilibrium strategy is $b(v) = \frac{12}{13}v$. [cite: 329]
