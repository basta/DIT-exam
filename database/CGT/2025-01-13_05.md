# Question
(5 pts) Find a symmetric equilibrium in a first-price auction with 5 bidders whose independent private valuations are drawn from the power distribution $F(x) = x^2$ where $x \in [0, 1]$[cite: 82].

---

# Solution
The optimal bidding strategy in a first-price auction with $n$ bidders is $b(v) = E[Y | Y < v]$, where $Y$ is the maximum valuation among the other $n-1$ bidders[cite: 86].

1. **CDF of the highest valuation among 4 opponents:**
   Since valuations are independent, $F_Y(x) = [F(x)]^{n-1} = (x^2)^{5-1} = x^8$[cite: 86].
2. **Strategy formula:**
   $b(v) = v - \int_{0}^{v} \frac{F_Y(x)}{F_Y(v)} dx$ [cite: 86]
3. **Calculation:**
   $$b(v) = v - \frac{1}{v^8} \int_{0}^{v} x^8 dx$$ [cite: 87]
   $$b(v) = v - \frac{1}{v^8} \left[ \frac{x^9}{9} \right]_{0}^{v}$$ [cite: 87]
   $$b(v) = v - \frac{v^9}{9v^8} = v - \frac{v}{9}$$ [cite: 87]
   $$b(v) = \frac{8v}{9}$$ [cite: 87]

**Final Answer:** The symmetric equilibrium bidding strategy is $b(v) = \frac{8}{9}v$.
